About the Film

Chris is excited to spend a weekend with his girlfriend Rose and meet her family for the first time. What begins as a perfect romantic getaway soon becomes a series of awkward family moments, unexpected surprises, and hilarious misunderstandings. Together, they must overcome every obstacle and discover that true love is worth fighting for.

Behind the Project

Why I Chose Get Out

I chose Get Out almost immediately because it is one of the movies that impressed me the most. I know the story very well, which made it easier for me to imagine a completely different version of the film. I believed the story could be transformed into a romantic comedy about a couple overcoming misunderstandings before finally finding their way back to each other.

Creating the Trailer

The trailer was the most difficult and time-consuming part of this project. Re-editing nearly two hours of footage into a two-minute romantic comedy trailer was a completely new experience for me. At first, I spent a long time searching for background music but couldn't find anything that matched my ideas. Instead, I started by selecting scenes from the original movie and reorganizing them into a new storyline. Later, while watching short videos online, I found a piece of music that perfectly matched the atmosphere I wanted. I then edited every shot according to the rhythm of the music and slowly completed the trailer.

Building the Website

Creating the website was the part I felt most confident about because it is closely related to my major in computer science. After finishing the trailer and posters, I realized I didn't want to submit them as separate files. Instead, I wanted to present everything together in one clean and professional website, similar to an official movie page. I chose a simple layout, soft colors, and elegant typography to match the romantic comedy style while keeping the website easy to navigate.

Reflection

This project helped me realize that genre is not only created by the story itself but also by the way a film is presented. By changing the music, editing, posters, and website design, the same movie can create completely different expectations for the audience. Through this project, I gained a better understanding of genre conventions and the importance of film marketing.
